Data Sources

This project draws on national and state-level surveys that track adolescent health and behaviors, including:

  • The Youth Risk Behavior Surveillance System (YRBSS), which measures sadness, hopelessness, and suicidal ideation.
  • Monitoring the Future (MTF), which tracks attitudes, substance use, and some digital behaviors among 12th graders.
  • Other youth surveys that include questions on sleep, online bullying, and screen time when available.

Interpreting the findings

In your own words, describe what stands out in the data. For example, you might highlight that persistent sadness is especially common among certain groups, or that students who sleep less report worse mental health outcomes. The goal is to connect descriptive statistics to the argument that we need a more systematic digital well-being data infrastructure.
