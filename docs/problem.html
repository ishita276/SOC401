Background

U.S. adolescents are experiencing record levels of sadness and hopelessness. National surveys show steep rises in anxiety, depression, and suicidal thoughts. These trends predate COVID-19 but have worsened since.

How AI and Social Media Intensify Risk

  • Algorithms amplify emotionally charged or harmful content.
  • Comparison culture increases shame, stress, and body-image issues.
  • Recommendation systems can push youth deeper into negative spiral content.

Adolescents—especially girls, LGBTQ+ youth, and students of color—face additional harms from harassment, bias, and discrimination embedded in online systems.

Data Gaps

Public institutions lack detailed data on youth digital behavior. Platforms possess rich behavioral datasets but do not share them in standardized, usable formats. Without timely data, policymakers cannot evaluate digital risks or regulate effectively.
