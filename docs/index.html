Executive Summary

Over the last decade, rates of sadness, anxiety, and self-harm among young people in the United States have risen sharply. During the same period, adolescents have spent increasing amounts of time inside AI-driven digital environments - especially social media platforms designed to maximize engagement rather than well-being.

This project brings together national survey data, interactive dashboards, and policy analysis to show how heavy digital exposure intersects with student well-being and access to mental health supports. It argues that current systems lack the timely, student-centered data needed to respond effectively, and proposes a Federal Youth Digital Well-Being Data Initiative to help policymakers better identify risk, target resources, and protect youth mental health.
