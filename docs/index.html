Executive Summary

Over the last decade, rates of sadness, anxiety, and self-harm among young people in the United States have risen sharply. At the same time, adolescents spend more of their lives inside AI-driven digital environments - especially social media platforms optimized for engagement rather than well-being.

This website presents evidence that current systems are failing young people, explains how algorithms intensify harm, and argues for a Federal Youth Digital Well-Being Data Initiative to give policymakers the real-time information needed to protect youth.
