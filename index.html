Executive Summary

Over the last decade, rates of sadness, anxiety, and self-harm among young people in the United States have risen sharply. At the same time, adolescents are spending more of their daily lives inside AI-driven digital environments – especially social media platforms that constantly optimize for attention and engagement rather than well-being.

This website argues that youth mental health should not depend on opaque algorithms and unregulated data practices. It presents evidence that current systems are failing young people, describes how AI-powered recommendation systems can intensify harm, and makes the case for a federal Youth Digital Well-Being Data Initiative that would give policymakers timely, integrated, and youth-centered information about digital risks.

What this site does

  • The Problem: Explains how AI-driven platforms intersect with existing youth mental health crises.
  • Evidence & Data: Highlights key descriptive statistics from national surveys and other data sources.
  • Policy Solution: Compares policy options and advocates for a federal digital well-being data infrastructure.
  • Take Action: Shares a student perspective and concrete steps different stakeholders can take.

The goal is not to “blame phones” for every problem in young people’s lives. Instead, this project focuses on the policy choices we can control: what data governments collect, how platforms are held accountable, and whether youth voices are centered in decisions about their digital futures.
